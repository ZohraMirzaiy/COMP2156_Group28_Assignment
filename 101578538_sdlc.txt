DevOps plays an important role in the software development life cycle (SDLC) because it helps connect development, testing, deployment, and maintenance into one smoother flow. Instead of seeing each phase as something separate, DevOps encourages teams to think about the whole process from start to finish.

This makes it easier to deliver updates more consistently and reduces the waiting time between stages, especially when teams are working on frequent changes.

One of the main benefits of DevOps in the SDLC is that it improves both speed and reliability at the same time. With automated testing and deployment pipelines, teams can release updates faster without feeling like they are taking big risks every time they push a change.

Instead of waiting until the very end to find problems, issues can be detected earlier in the process. This makes the overall development cycle more stable and less stressful for the team.
